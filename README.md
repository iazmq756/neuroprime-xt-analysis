## The 9 Ingredients Inside NeuroPrime XT — What the Research Says

This is where things get interesting. A lot of "brain supplements" pile in a handful of well-known names (Ginkgo, B vitamins) and call it a day. NeuroPrime XT takes a different approach, stacking nine ingredients that each target different aspects of cognitive health.

**Tamarind** — Sometimes called the "miracle detox fruit," tamarind is rich in polyphenols and antioxidants. It's associated with helping the body flush fluoride. That's the core pineal detox claim, and tamarind is the ingredient most directly linked to it.

**Lion's Mane Mushroom** — Probably the most well-studied ingredient here. Lion's Mane contains hericenones and erinacines — compounds that have been studied for their effects on nerve growth factor (NGF). A 2009 study in *Phytotherapy Research* observed improvements in cognitive assessments among adults with mild cognitive concerns who took Lion's Mane extract versus a placebo.

**Moringa** — Nutrient-dense and packed with antioxidants. Research points to its role in reducing oxidative stress in the brain, which is one of the primary drivers of age-related cognitive decline. It also supports melatonin production.

**French Maritime Pine Bark Extract** — Sourced from trees on France's southwest coast. Known to improve blood flow and increase melatonin levels, which can help with sleep quality — and better sleep means better memory consolidation.

**Spirulina** — An ancient algae used across civilizations. Research suggests it may protect against neurodegenerative conditions and help remove heavy metals from the brain.

**Chlorella** — Another algae, this time from Okinawa. Used to boost natural melatonin production and reduce oxidative brain stress.

**Maidenhair Tree Extract (Ginkgo Biloba)** — One of the oldest trees on earth. Known for enhancing cerebral blood flow, which delivers oxygen and nutrients to brain cells. Widely studied for its effects on memory and concentration.

**Bacopa Monnieri** — An Ayurvedic herb with a long history of use for learning and memory. Modern research has explored its role in memory retention and recall, particularly over extended periods of use.

**Neem** — Often overlooked in Western supplements. Traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties, particularly for supporting neural repair.

Plain summary: the ingredient list is diverse and each component has independent research support. Whether they work synergistically in this specific formula, at this specific dosage, is harder to verify — but there's no obvious filler in the mix.
